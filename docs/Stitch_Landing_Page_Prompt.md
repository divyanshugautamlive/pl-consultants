# Stitch Prompt — Pinnacle Logic Consulting Single-Page Landing Page

Copy everything below into Stitch as a single prompt. **Attach the Pinnacle Logic Consulting logo file (`Pinnacle Logo.png`) alongside this prompt** so Stitch extracts exact colors from the real mark instead of guessing from a text description.

---

You are a senior UI/UX designer and conversion-focused prompt engineer designing **one single-page landing page** (not a multi-page site) for a B2B manufacturing consulting brand, anchored to an existing logo (attached). Do not default to a generic navy-and-gold corporate-consulting template just because that's the palette — those colors already carry specific brand meaning (see mark description below). Your job is to build a distinctive, credible, conversion-first scrolling page around them. Before generating screens, briefly state the visual direction you've chosen — how you're extending the logo's mark and palette into a single-page system — and why it fits the brand below.

## Brand mark to anchor the design

**Full company name: Pinnacle Logic Consulting.** Use the full name in the header lockup and footer — never abbreviate on first mention.

The logo (attached) is a circular navy swoosh enclosing an ascending steel-gray bar chart with a gold arrow sweeping upward through it — a visual metaphor for uncovering and accelerating growth. Wordmark: "PINNACLE LOGIC" in gold + navy, with "CONSULTING" in a smaller navy tracked-out subheading beneath, flanked by thin gold rules. Tagline: "Unlock Hidden Potential. Create Lasting Impact."

Treat this as the literal source of truth for:
- **Color system** — deep navy, warm gold/brass, steel gray; extract exact hex values from the attached file rather than guessing.
- **Iconography language** — simple, geometric, line/solid hybrid icons.
- **Tagline** as a recurring motif (hero subline, section dividers, footer).

## Business context

**Company:** Pinnacle Logic Consulting — a solo-led manufacturing operations consultancy in India.
**Founder:** Tarun Sharma, Lean Six Sigma-certified Operational Excellence Consultant.
**What they do:** Recover hidden profit inside factories (₹5–30 Cr typical) by fixing OEE, changeover time, line balancing, manpower waste, and lead-time — without clients spending on new machinery. Approach, shown in condensed form only: **Diagnose → Quantify → Execute → Sustain.**

**Proof points to design around (give these visual weight — large-scale numerals, dedicated stat components — not paragraph text):**
- ₹43.42 Cr+ in business value identified/created across 5 engagements
- 5 case studies across FMCG, Automotive & Electronics, FIBC & Bulk Bag/Packaging, Integrated Steel, Wire & Cable
- Typical factory OEE: 40–60% (recoverable to 75%+)
- 15–35% recoverable manpower waste, 20–40% possible lead-time compression
- <6 months average payback period

**Brand personality:** Expert, data-driven, credible, no-nonsense. Not flashy, not "startup," not generic-corporate. This should feel like it belongs to someone who spends their day on a factory floor with a stopwatch and a P&L, not in a boardroom. Confidence comes from data density and precision, not decoration. The gold arrow is the one permitted flourish — use gold sparingly and intentionally (₹ figures, key CTAs, the ascending-arrow motif), not as a general accent.

**Target audience (has to trust this page in 10 seconds):**
- Primary: COOs, Plant Heads, Operations Managers at manufacturing companies (₹10–100 Cr revenue)
- Secondary: CFOs / Finance Leads at PE-backed manufacturers

**The page's single job:** Convert a skeptical, busy operations leader into a booked shopfloor assessment call — everything on the page should point at that one action.

## Messaging discipline to reflect visually

- Every claim is denominated in rupees or hard numbers, never vague percentages alone.
- Lead with business impact, not process jargon.
- Emphasize hands-on shopfloor execution (Tarun physically on-site) over "advisory from an office."
- Data over decoration — this audience trusts evidence, not adjectives.
- Do **not** show ROI multiples ("10x–50x") or week/duration estimates anywhere on the page — those have been intentionally removed from this brand's messaging.

## Format constraint: this is ONE page, not a site

Design a single continuous scroll (desktop + mobile), roughly the length of 2–3 printed pages when read top to bottom. No secondary pages, no multi-level navigation — the nav is just in-page anchor links. Every section below must appear once, in this order, on this one page.

## Required sections (functional spec — design freely within the brand system above, but include all nine, in order)

1. **Sticky header** — logo (linked to top), in-page anchor nav (Services, Results, Approach, Contact), a persistent "Book Assessment" CTA button that stays visible on scroll, plus click-to-call and WhatsApp icons for mobile.

2. **Hero** — headline "Unlock Hidden Capacity, Productivity & Profit"; subheadline on ₹5–30 Cr recovery potential, no new capex, <6-month payback; primary CTA "Start Your Shopfloor Assessment"; secondary CTA WhatsApp chat link; a compact trust strip (₹43.42 Cr+ created · 5 engagements · 5 sectors).

3. **Problem statement** — 3–4 stat callouts (typical OEE 40–60%, 15–35% recoverable manpower waste, 20–40% lead-time compression, 20–30% of costs are recoverable waste) with one short framing line: factories are quietly leaving profit on the table.

4. **Approach (condensed)** — a 4-step visual flow, Diagnose → Quantify → Execute → Sustain, each step just a name + one-line description. No activity sub-lists, no deliverable bullets — headline + one line only.

5. **Results / social proof** — headline stat block (₹43.42 Cr+ created & identified, 5 engagements) plus all 5 case study preview cards (sector, one-line challenge, ₹ impact) — show all 5, no partial slice.

6. **Why Pinnacle Logic Consulting** — all 6 differentiators, exact copy: rupee-denominated outcomes; hands-on shopfloor execution; data-driven problem solving; Lean Six Sigma methodology; manufacturing domain expertise; sustainable results locked in through standards & KPIs.

7. **Sectors served** — explicit tag/pill row or icon strip: FMCG, Automotive & Electronics, FIBC & Bulk Bag/Packaging, Integrated Steel, Wire & Cable.

8. **Lead capture band** — short form (name, company, email, phone, role dropdown, main-challenge dropdown with an Others free-text option), with parallel WhatsApp and direct-call CTAs alongside the form, and a clear success/error state.

9. **Footer** — full company name + logo, contact details (email, phone, LinkedIn), Privacy/Terms links, copyright line.
