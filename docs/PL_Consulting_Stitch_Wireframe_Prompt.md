## Business context

**Company:** Pinnacle Logic Consulting — a solo-led manufacturing operations consultancy in India.
**Founder:** Tarun Sharma, Lean Six Sigma-certified Operational Excellence Consultant.
**What they do:** Recover hidden profit inside factories (₹5–30 Cr typical) by fixing OEE, changeover time, line balancing, manpower waste, and lead-time — without clients spending on new machinery. Delivery is a hands-on 5-phase engagement called **DADES**: Diagnose → Analyze → Design → Execute → Sustain.

**Proof points to weave into the design (not just copy — treat these as first-class visual content, e.g. stat callouts, big numbers):**
- ₹43.42 Cr+ in business value identified/created across engagements
- 10x–50x typical client ROI
- <6 months average payback period
- 5+ case studies across FMCG, Auto, Electronics, Steel, Wire & Cable sectors
- Typical factory OEE: 40–60% (recoverable to 75%+)
- 15–35% recoverable manpower waste
- 20–40% possible lead-time compression

**Brand personality:** Expert, data-driven, credible, no-nonsense. Not flashy, not "startup," not generic-corporate either — this should feel like it belongs to someone who spends their day on a factory floor with a stopwatch and a P&L, not in a boardroom. Confidence should come from data density and precision, not decoration. The gold arrow in the mark is the one permitted flourish — use gold sparingly and intentionally (ROI numbers, key CTAs, the ascending-arrow motif) rather than as a general accent.

**Target audience (who has to trust this site in 10 seconds):**
- Primary: COOs, Plant Heads, Operations Managers at manufacturing companies (₹10–100 Cr revenue)
- Secondary: CFOs / Finance Leads at PE-backed manufacturers
- Tertiary: Potential hires/partners evaluating credibility

**Website's single job:** Convert a skeptical, busy operations leader into a booked shopfloor assessment call — nothing else matters more than this conversion path.

## Messaging discipline to reflect visually

- Every claim is denominated in rupees or hard numbers, never vague percentages alone — design should give numbers visual weight (large scale, dedicated components), not bury them in paragraphs.
- Lead with business impact, not process jargon.
- Emphasize hands-on shopfloor execution (Tarun physically on-site) over "advisory from an office."
- Data over decoration — this audience trusts evidence, not adjectives.
